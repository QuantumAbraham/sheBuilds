When students imagine data and AI careers, the distance between a course assignment and a real role can feel enormous.

But most journeys are built from smaller moments.

## It often starts with one project that feels different

Maybe it is a classification notebook that suddenly makes sense. Maybe it is a visualization that helps someone understand a messy problem. Maybe it is the first time a student realizes she enjoys the mix of logic, experimentation, and storytelling.

That spark matters.

## Confidence grows through repetition and feedback

The first model rarely feels elegant. The point is to keep shipping the next experiment.

```python
features = build_features(student_feedback)
model.fit(features, labels)
predictions = model.predict(validation_set)
```

Even a tiny workflow like this can become more meaningful when a community helps students ask better questions about data quality, ethics, debugging, and impact.

## Community widens the path

Students in data and AI benefit from seeing researchers, ML engineers, product thinkers, and analysts in the same conversation. It reminds them that "working in AI" is not one narrow identity.

She Builds aims to hold that wider picture open.
