People often imagine mentorship as a big, formal structure.

Sometimes it is. But often the most helpful mentoring moments are much smaller.

## Good mentoring reduces friction

A mentor can help a student translate a vague worry into a specific action:

- which internship applications are realistic right now
- how to talk about a course project with more confidence
- which skills are worth focusing on this month
- whether a student is actually "behind" or simply early in the process

## Role models matter because they make the future less blurry

Students do not only need advice. They need evidence that there are many successful ways to build a technical life.

That is why She Builds wants mentoring to feel approachable, warm, and grounded in honest stories rather than perfection.

## What we want mentoring to feel like

Not pressure.

Not performance.

A clearer next step, a little more courage, and the feeling that someone farther along has made room for you.
