The room filled gradually, then all at once.

Laptops opened. Sticky notes appeared. Someone asked for help with a React bug. Another student was sketching a user flow for a study companion. A pair at the back started whiteboarding an API idea.

## What made the night work

It was not only the projects. It was the permission structure around them.

Students did not need finished ideas. They could arrive with a messy concept, a half-working prototype, or simply the desire to sit near other people who were trying.

## Momentum came from shared visibility

When everyone puts their work out into the room, progress becomes contagious. One student finishing a small piece encourages another student to keep going instead of closing her laptop early.

## The real takeaway

A good event does more than fill an evening. It gives students a more believable next chapter.

That is the kind of momentum She Builds wants to create repeatedly.
